Recall \parencite[Dfn 2.15]{lesnik2021synthetictopologyconstructivemetric} that a subset $K\subseteq Y$ is subcompact in $Y$ if for every $U\subseteq Y$ open, the statement $K \subseteq U$ is open. 

Note immediately that all closed subsets of a compact Hausdorff space are subcompact, as they are themselves compact Hausdorff and thus compact.

A set $Y$ is called locally compact (\parencite{p59}[lesnik2021synthetictopologyconstructivemetric] if for every $U\subseteq Y$ open and $x\in U$ there exists an open $V\subseteq Y$ and subcompact $K\subseteq Y$ such that $x\in V \subseteq K \subseteq U$. 

Note that Stone spaces are locally compact, as all opens are unions of clopens, any which containing $x$ will work for both $V$ and $K$. 

Also note that the unit interval is locally compact, which is good enough for the application i care about \parencite[Prop 2.58]{lesnik2021synthetictopologyconstructivemetric}.

Are compact Hausdorff spaces also locally compact? 
Let $X:\CHaus$, presented by $q:S \twoheadrightarrow X$ and $U\subseteq X$ open with $x\in U$. By \parencite[closedInCHaus]{synthetic-stone-duality}, $U$ can be written as $\bigcup_{n:\mathbb N} \neg q(D_n)$, where $D_n$ a sequence of decidable subsets of $S$. As $x\in U$, there merely exists some $n$ with $x \in \neg q(D_n)$. Let $V= \neg q(D_n)$ and $K = q(\neg D_n)$.
